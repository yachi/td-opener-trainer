## Overview
Mountainous Stacking (also known as Mountain Stacking or ITO Cannon) is a Triple Double opener that results in a T-Spin Triple in the second bag, then a Perfect Clear chance in the third, as well as a chance to get a T-Spin Double and Perfect Clear within the third bag.

## First Bag (MS original)
For early I and L/J starts in the first bag, one can use this Triple Double opener. The J piece (L piece in the mirrored setup) would be on hold. Overall setup rate: 53.33% with mirror / 33.33% without.

## MS — Second Bag and Beyond
### PC after 6 lines
- **Triple Double Perfect Clear**: place L piece after TST. Overall PC rate: 7.14%, ideally with a TSD first.
- **Triple Single Perfect Clear**: easier. O before S and J, hold L. PC rate: 21.43%. Fallback to 8 lines: 96.55%.

### Compromise (MS)
0.56% chance. 44.80% PC rate. If fails, stack depending on I, Z, J, L order — 35.71% PC after 10 lines.

## MS2 (successor)
Like its predecessor, a Triple Double opener **mainly intended for a Perfect Clear in the third bag**. The L piece (J in the mirrored opener) is on hold until the second bag. 100% stack rate with hold + softdrops + mirror, or 66.67% regular only.

### MS2 Compromise
0.75% chance. Continuation: third bag always stackable via Z and J order; fourth bag → 100% PC chance.

### Setup 2C loop
S before O → 2C. Third bag: place all pieces, then held I on SZO. Fourth bag: L-Spin to place L after O, J. **100% PC after 6 more lines (14 total).**

## MS3
Similar to Bacon's Maple Stacking except O piece kept on hold. Use with J/L start. 86.67% stack with mirror, 66.67% regular.

### MS3 PC after 8 lines
| Setup | Setup rate | PC rate | Minimals | Condition |

| D | 75% | 95.91% | 18 | if must place O |

### MS3 Compromise
Covers bags that don't allow PC setup. Only TSD in TST form. Setup lasts until 5th bag.
